# Citation Styles Reference

Used during Pass 2 and Pass 5 of the humanize-research skill (primarily Mode D). This file documents the three major academic citation styles, adapted to Walied's no-em-dash and minimal-colon rules. Use whichever style the target journal or venue requires. Default is Harvard Author-Date.

## Style selection

| Venue type | Default style |
|---|---|
| Management, business, entrepreneurship journals | Harvard Author-Date or APA 7th |
| Information systems journals | APA 7th |
| Economics journals (NBER, AEA) | Chicago Author-Date |
| Public policy journals | APA 7th |
| Conference papers (WASD, AOM, ICIS) | Match conference template (usually APA 7th) |
| Walied's default if unspecified | Harvard Author-Date |

## 1. Harvard Author-Date (Walied-adapted)

The most common style in UK and European management journals. Adapted to use commas and "p." rather than colons for page numbers.

### 1.1 In-text citation forms

| Citation type | Format |
|---|---|
| Single author paraphrase | (Eisenmann 2013) |
| Single author with page | (Eisenmann 2013, p. 45) |
| Two authors | (Eisenmann and Bingham 2018, p. 102) |
| Three or more authors | (Eisenmann et al. 2013, p. 45) |
| Multiple sources, same point | (Eisenmann 2013; Bingham 2018; Brown 2024) |
| Same author, multiple years | (Eisenmann 2013, 2018) |
| Same author and year | (Eisenmann 2013a; 2013b) |
| Direct quote | "quoted text" (Eisenmann 2013, p. 45) |
| Block quote (40+ words) | indented, no quote marks, citation at end (Eisenmann 2013, p. 45) |
| Secondary citation | (Smith 2010, cited in Jones 2023, p. 102) |
| Corporate author | (World Bank 2024, p. 17) |
| No date | (Anthropic n.d.) |
| Personal communication | (W. Truell, personal communication, 13 November 2025) [not in reference list] |

### 1.2 Reference list format

Alphabetical by surname of first author. Hanging indent. Double-spaced (or single-spaced for working papers).

#### Journal article

Eisenmann, T., 2013. Entrepreneurship: A working definition. *Harvard Business Review*, 91(1), pp. 23-25. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx

#### Book

Blank, S., 2013. *The Four Steps to the Epiphany. Successful strategies for products that win.* 2nd ed. Pescadero, CA: K&S Ranch.

Note. Standard Harvard would be "The Four Steps to the Epiphany: Successful strategies..." with a colon. Walied-adapted form replaces with a period.

#### Book chapter

Carbrey, J., 2024. Studio equity allocation under AI-native production. In: B. Yoskovitz and S. Foley, eds. *The Venture Studio Handbook*. London: Routledge, pp. 102-127.

#### Edited book

Yoskovitz, B. and Foley, S., eds., 2024. *The Venture Studio Handbook*. London: Routledge.

#### Working paper

Tunguz, T., 2026. *The Org Chart Math Behind AI-Native Speed*. Working Paper. Available at https://tomtunguz.com/communication-tax-small-orgs/ [accessed 27 April 2026].

#### Conference paper

Elbashir, W. and Dafaalla, A., 2026. Augmented intelligence in UAE school transport. *Proceedings of the WASD Sustainable Development World Conference 2026*, London, 24-26 June 2026, pp. 145-162.

#### Government report

OECD, 2025. *Venture Capital and Innovation in MENA. Trends 2024-2026*. Paris: OECD Publishing. https://doi.org/10.1787/xxxxxxxx

#### News article

Field, H., 2025. AI startup Cursor raises $2.3 billion at $29.3 billion valuation. *CNBC*, [online] 13 November. Available at https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/13/cursor-ai-startup-funding-round-valuation.html [accessed 27 April 2026].

#### Web page

METR, 2026. *Time Horizon 1.1*. [online] Available at https://metr.org/blog/2026-1-29-time-horizon-1-1/ [accessed 27 April 2026].

#### Personal correspondence

Not listed in the reference list. Cited in-text only.

### 1.3 Page ranges

Default. Use a hyphen, not an en-dash. "pp. 45-67".

If the target journal explicitly demands en-dashes, flag for final adjustment at submission.

### 1.4 DOI handling

DOIs are mandatory where available. Format. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx

If a DOI is not available but the work is online, use the URL plus access date.

If the work is offline-only (book chapter without DOI), no link is required.

## 2. APA 7th Edition (Walied-adapted)

Most common in psychology, education, and information systems. Many management journals (notably MIS Quarterly, Information & Management) require APA 7th.

### 2.1 In-text citation forms

| Citation type | Format |
|---|---|
| Single author paraphrase | (Eisenmann, 2013) |
| Single author with page | (Eisenmann, 2013, p. 45) |
| Two authors | (Eisenmann & Bingham, 2018) |
| Three or more authors | (Eisenmann et al., 2013) |
| Multiple sources | (Eisenmann, 2013; Bingham, 2018) |
| Direct quote | "quoted text" (Eisenmann, 2013, p. 45) |
| Corporate author first cite | (World Bank [WB], 2024) |
| Corporate author later cite | (WB, 2024) |

Note the comma before the year in APA. Harvard does not use one.

### 2.2 Reference list format

Hanging indent. APA 7th uses sentence case for article and book titles (only the first word and proper nouns capitalised in titles, not headline case).

#### Journal article

Eisenmann, T. (2013). Entrepreneurship. A working definition. *Harvard Business Review*, *91*(1), 23-25. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx

Note that the period after "Entrepreneurship" is a Walied adaptation. Standard APA uses a colon there.

#### Book

Blank, S. (2013). *The four steps to the epiphany. Successful strategies for products that win* (2nd ed.). K&S Ranch.

#### Book chapter

Carbrey, J. (2024). Studio equity allocation under AI-native production. In B. Yoskovitz & S. Foley (Eds.), *The venture studio handbook* (pp. 102-127). Routledge.

#### Online article

Field, H. (2025, November 13). AI startup Cursor raises $2.3 billion at $29.3 billion valuation. *CNBC*. https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/13/cursor-ai-startup-funding-round-valuation.html

### 2.3 APA-specific notes

- DOI placement at end of reference, no period after.
- Up to 20 authors listed before "..." and final author.
- Use ampersand "&" between authors in reference list, "and" in text.

## 3. Chicago Author-Date (Walied-adapted)

Common in economics and history journals.

### 3.1 In-text citation forms

| Citation type | Format |
|---|---|
| Single author | (Eisenmann 2013) |
| Single author with page | (Eisenmann 2013, 45) |
| Two authors | (Eisenmann and Bingham 2018, 45) |
| Three or more authors | (Eisenmann et al. 2013, 45) |

Note. Standard Chicago uses no comma between author and year. Walied-adapted form keeps that. For page reference, standard Chicago uses just a comma (no "p."). Both forms are accepted; Walied-adapted form follows standard Chicago here.

### 3.2 Reference list format

#### Journal article

Eisenmann, Thomas. 2013. "Entrepreneurship. A Working Definition." *Harvard Business Review* 91 (1). 23-25. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx

Note. Standard Chicago uses a colon between volume-issue and page. Walied-adapted uses a period.

#### Book

Blank, Steve. 2013. *The Four Steps to the Epiphany. Successful Strategies for Products That Win*. 2nd ed. Pescadero, CA. K&S Ranch.

## 4. Common pitfalls and reviewer pet peeves

Citations are where many otherwise good papers lose marks in review. Avoid these.

### 4.1 Inconsistent style mid-paper

Pick one style and apply it everywhere. Reviewers notice. They will list every inconsistency.

### 4.2 Missing DOIs

If a journal article has a DOI and the citation does not include it, the reference is incomplete by 2024 standards.

### 4.3 Outdated URLs without access dates

Web sources without access dates fail review at most journals. Always include access date in the form (accessed DD Month YYYY).

### 4.4 Hallucinated journal titles or page numbers

LLMs frequently invent these. Citation verification (Pass 1) catches them, but they sneak back in if you re-edit references after Pass 1. Re-verify after every edit pass.

### 4.5 Wrong author order

LLMs sometimes reorder authors alphabetically when they should be in original-publication order. Always preserve the published author order.

### 4.6 Missing "et al." rules

Different styles have different "et al." thresholds. Harvard uses et al. for 3+. APA 7th uses et al. for 3+ in citations but lists up to 20 in references. Chicago uses et al. for 4+ in text but lists up to 10 in references. Apply consistently.

### 4.7 Page numbers in non-pagedied sources

Web pages and most blogs do not have page numbers. Do not invent them. Cite by paragraph or section if precision is needed ("para. 4" or "Discussion section").

## 5. Quick verification checklist for the reference list

Before finalising any academic submission, run through this checklist.

1. Is every in-text citation present in the reference list?
2. Is every reference-list entry cited at least once in the text?
3. Are author names consistent (no Smith vs Smyth confusion)?
4. Are years consistent (no 2023 vs 2024 disagreement between text and reference list)?
5. Are DOIs included where available?
6. Do all URLs resolve? (Random sample test 5 to 10 URLs in any 50-reference list.)
7. Is the reference list in alphabetical order by first author surname?
8. Are the formatting rules (italics for journal titles, sentence case for APA, etc.) consistent?
9. Are page ranges using the same dash convention (hyphen for Walied-adapted, en-dash if reverting to standard)?
10. Is the citation style internally consistent (no mixing Harvard and APA)?

A 50-reference paper deserves 30 to 60 minutes on this checklist alone. Reviewers will catch what you miss.
