# Estonian Corporate Law Reference

## Primary Legislation
- **Estonian Commercial Code** (*Äriseadustik*, ÄS) — governs OÜ (private limited company), AS (public limited company), partnerships
- **General Part of the Civil Code Act** (*Tsiviilseadustiku üldosa seadus*, TsÜS) — general principles of civil law, legal capacity, representation
- **Law of Obligations Act** (*Võlaõigusseadus*, VÕS) — contract law, tort law, unjust enrichment
- **Taxation Act** (*Maksukorralduse seadus*) and **Income Tax Act** (*Tulumaksuseadus*) — unique distributed profits tax

## OÜ (Osaühing) — Private Limited Company

### Formation
- Minimum share capital: **EUR 0.01** (since February 2023; previously EUR 2,500)
- Legacy agreements and articles referencing EUR 2,500 minimum should be updated
- Can be formed entirely digitally via e-Residency / e-Business Register
- Articles of association (*põhikiri*) filed with the Commercial Register (*Äriregister*)
- At least one management board member (*juhatus*) required; need not be Estonian resident
- No requirement for a supervisory board (*nõukogu*) unless articles provide otherwise or share capital exceeds EUR 25,000

### Share Structure
- Shares (*osad*) are not securities — they are participations recorded in the Commercial Register
- Each shareholder has one share; the share has a nominal value
- Shares can have different nominal values → different voting rights (proportional to nominal value by default)
- **Share transfer** requires notarized agreement OR digital signing via Estonian ID/e-Residency card
- Share transfer must be entered in the Commercial Register to be effective against third parties

### Pre-emption Rights (Critical)
- **Statutory pre-emption**: Under ÄS § 149, other shareholders have a right of first refusal on any share transfer to third parties
- Transfer contracts that violate pre-emption rights are **VOID** — not merely voidable
- The right can be **modified or excluded** in the articles of association
- If excluded in articles, the SHA should reflect this; if the SHA grants pre-emption but the articles don't, the SHA provision may be unenforceable against third parties
- Exercise period: 2 months from notification (unless articles provide otherwise)
- Pre-emption right does NOT apply to transfers between existing shareholders (unless articles say otherwise)

### Management Board (*Juhatus*)
- One or more members; appointed by shareholders (general meeting) or supervisory board if one exists
- Term: up to 3 years (can be extended by articles)
- Board members have unlimited personal liability for breach of duty of care and loyalty
- Board members can be removed at any time by the appointing body, with or without cause (but employment-like protections may apply if a service contract exists)
- Transactions between the company and a board member require shareholder approval

### Shareholder Meetings & Decisions
- Ordinary general meeting: at least once per year (to approve annual report)
- Decisions can be made without a meeting — written procedure (ÄS § 173)
- Simple majority (>50%) for ordinary resolutions
- **2/3 majority** required for: amending articles, increasing/decreasing share capital, mergers, divisions, transformations
- **Unanimous consent** required for: restricting shareholders' rights, imposing additional obligations on shareholders
- Minority rights: shareholders holding at least 1/10 of share capital can demand a general meeting, demand inclusion of items on the agenda, and demand information from the board

### Shareholder Agreements (SHA)
- SHAs are contractual — they bind only the parties, not the company itself
- SHA provisions that conflict with the articles of association may be unenforceable against the company or third parties
- Key SHA provisions to always include:
  - Transfer restrictions (drag-along, tag-along, ROFR, lock-up)
  - Board composition and appointment rights
  - Reserved matters requiring supermajority or unanimous consent
  - Information rights beyond statutory minimums
  - Non-compete obligations for key shareholders
  - Deadlock resolution mechanisms
  - Exit provisions (put/call options, valuation methodology)
- SHA should always be aligned with articles of association — if the SHA grants rights that require articles amendment, ensure articles are amended simultaneously

### Dividends & Distributions
- Dividends can only be paid from net profit or retained earnings (after covering losses)
- The board proposes dividend; shareholders approve at general meeting
- **Tax**: 22% CIT on distributed profits (effective rate ~28.21% on pre-tax profit equivalent)
- Regular dividend distributions qualify for a reduced rate of 14% (on the 1/3 of distributions that don't exceed the average of the prior 3 years' distributions)
- Interim dividends are permitted if the articles allow and there is sufficient net profit from the current financial year

### Liability
- OÜ has separate legal personality — shareholders' liability limited to their contribution
- **Piercing the corporate veil** is possible but extremely rare under Estonian law — requires abuse of legal personality, deliberate undercapitalization for the purpose of harming creditors, or confusion of company and personal assets
- Board members' personal liability: fiduciary duties of care and loyalty; can be held liable by the company, and in insolvency, by creditors

## Estonian Tax System Essentials

### Corporate Income Tax
- **0% on retained earnings** — unique globally
- **22% on distributed profits** (dividends, fringe benefits, gifts, non-business expenditures)
- Reduced rate of **14%** on recurring distributions (up to 1/3 of average prior 3-year distributions)
- Withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents: 0% (if beneficial owner is EU parent company with 10%+ holding) or 7% (under the reduced rate) or various rates under double tax treaties

### Social Tax
- **33%** paid by employer on gross wages (20% pension + 13% health insurance)

### Double Tax Treaties
- Estonia has 60+ DTTs
- Key treaties for Intuitio context: Estonia-UAE (signed 2024), Estonia-Ethiopia (no treaty — check withholding implications), Estonia-US (treaty in force)

## Regulatory Considerations
- **AML/KYC**: Estonian Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing Prevention Act — stringent requirements
- **Financial services**: Estonian Financial Supervision Authority (Finantsinspektsioon) regulates banking, insurance, investment services
- **Virtual currency**: Requires specific license from Finantsinspektsioon (tightened significantly since 2022)
- **Data protection**: Estonian Data Protection Inspectorate (*Andmekaitse Inspektsioon*) enforces GDPR locally
