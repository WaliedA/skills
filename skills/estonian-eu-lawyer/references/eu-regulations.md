# EU Regulations Reference

## GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation — Regulation 2016/679)

### When It Applies
- Any processing of personal data by an EU-established controller or processor
- Any processing of EU data subjects' data by non-EU entities offering goods/services to EU or monitoring EU behavior
- **Key for context**: An Estonian OÜ is always subject to GDPR. Partners processing data on behalf of the OÜ must comply regardless of their jurisdiction.

### Core Principles (Art. 5)
1. Lawfulness, fairness, transparency
2. Purpose limitation
3. Data minimization
4. Accuracy
5. Storage limitation
6. Integrity and confidentiality
7. Accountability

### Legal Bases for Processing (Art. 6)
- Consent (freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous)
- Contract performance
- Legal obligation
- Vital interests
- Public interest
- Legitimate interests (balancing test)

### Data Processing Agreements (Art. 28) — Critical for Partnerships
When controller engages a processor:
- **Written DPA required** including: subject matter, duration, purpose, data types, data subject categories, processor obligations
- Processor must: process only on documented instructions, ensure confidentiality, implement security, assist with data subject requests, delete/return on termination, allow audits
- Sub-processing: only with prior authorization
- **International transfers** outside EEA require safeguards:
  - Adequacy decision (limited countries)
  - Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — most common
  - Binding Corporate Rules
  - **UAE**: No adequacy decision → SCCs required
  - **Ethiopia**: No adequacy decision → SCCs required
  - **US**: EU-US Data Privacy Framework (certified companies) or SCCs

### Breach Notification
- **72 hours** to notify supervisory authority
- Notify data subjects "without undue delay" if high risk
- Processor must notify controller "without undue delay"

### Penalties
- Up to EUR 20M or 4% of annual global turnover (serious violations)
- Estonian supervisory authority: *Andmekaitse Inspektsioon*

### GDPR in Agreements — Checklist
1. Define each party's controller/processor role
2. Specify personal data categories and data subjects
3. Require separate Data Processing Agreement
4. Address international transfers with safeguards
5. Include breach notification obligations
6. Address data subject rights cooperation
7. Include data return/deletion on termination

## EU Cross-Border Investment Regulations

### FDI Screening (Regulation 2019/452)
- Estonia has national screening mechanism (since 2023)
- Affects: critical infrastructure, critical technologies, defense, media, data processing

### Anti-Money Laundering (5th & 6th AML Directives)
- Estonia has strict AML regime (post-Danske Bank scandal)
- KYC/CDD requirements for all business relationships
- UBO registration mandatory

### Prospectus Regulation (2017/1129)
- Exemptions: qualified investors only, <150 persons per Member State, min EUR 100,000 investment

### AIFMD (Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive)
- Sub-threshold managers (<EUR 100M leveraged / <EUR 500M unleveraged) get lighter registration regime
- Marketing non-EU funds to EU investors: national private placement regimes apply

## EU Employment Directives

### Posted Workers (96/71/EC, revised 2018/957)
- Posted workers receive host country minimum conditions
- A1 certificate required

### Working Time (2003/88/EC)
- Max 48-hour average week; min 4 weeks paid leave; min 11 hours daily rest

## EU IP Harmonization

### Trade Secrets Directive (2016/943)
- Three conditions: (1) secret, (2) commercial value because secret, (3) reasonable steps to keep secret

### Software Directive (2009/24/EC)
- Employee-created software: economic rights vest in employer by default

## EU Sanctions
- Directly applicable in all Member States
- **Sudan**: Targeted sanctions (arms embargo, financial restrictions on designated persons)
- **Russia/Belarus**: Comprehensive sanctions
- Sanctions compliance clause essential for agreements covering sanctioned regions
- Estonian enforcement: *Rahapesu Andmebüroo* (Financial Intelligence Unit)
