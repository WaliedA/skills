# AIFMD Compliance Guide for Estonian Fund Managers

## Overview

The Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (2011/61/EU) — **AIFMD** — is the EU regulatory framework for managers of alternative investment funds (AIFs), including venture capital funds, private equity funds, hedge funds, and real estate funds. It is implemented in Estonia through the **Investment Funds Act** (*Investeerimisfondide seadus*).

The Estonian Financial Supervision Authority (**Finantsinspektsioon**, or **FI**) is the competent authority for AIFM registration and authorization in Estonia.

## Does AIFMD Apply to You?

### Definition of an AIF
An AIF is any collective investment undertaking that:
1. Raises capital from a number of investors
2. With a view to investing it in accordance with a defined investment policy
3. For the benefit of those investors

A venture capital fund or venture studio fund **is an AIF** under this definition. The Delaware LP managed by an Estonian OÜ GP falls squarely within AIFMD scope.

### Who Is the AIFM?
The AIFM (Alternative Investment Fund Manager) is the entity responsible for managing the AIF. In a typical structure:
- **Estonian GP OÜ** = the AIFM (if it performs portfolio management and risk management)
- If the management company is separate from the GP, the management company may be the AIFM
- Only one entity can be designated as the AIFM per fund

## Sub-Threshold Regime (Registration Only)

### Thresholds
You qualify for the lighter "sub-threshold" regime if your total AUM (across all managed AIFs) is below:
- **EUR 100 million** (if using leverage in any fund), OR
- **EUR 500 million** (if: no leverage in any fund AND no redemption rights for 5 years from initial investment)

For a venture studio fund of USD 3M with no leverage and a 10-year term: **you qualify for sub-threshold registration**.

### Registration Process

**Step 1: Notification to Finantsinspektsioon**

Submit a notification including:
1. Identity of the AIFM and its directors/board members
2. Identification of all AIFs managed
3. Information on investment strategies for each AIF
4. Information on main instruments traded
5. Information on principal exposures and most important concentrations
6. Arrangements for delegation (if any management functions delegated)
7. Information on any service providers (auditor, administrator, custodian)

**Step 2: Supporting Documentation**
- AIFM articles of association
- AIF offering documents (LPA, PPM)
- Description of organizational structure
- Compliance arrangements
- Business plan
- Fit and proper documentation for directors (CV, criminal record check, qualification evidence)

**Step 3: FI Review**
- FI reviews the notification (typically 30-60 days)
- FI may request additional information
- Registration confirmed by letter/notice
- No license fee for sub-threshold registration (verify current FI fee schedule)

### Ongoing Obligations (Sub-Threshold)

| Obligation | Frequency | Details |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| Annual reporting to FI | Annual | Report on: main instruments, principal exposures, concentrations, performance data |
| AML compliance | Ongoing | Full AML/KYC obligations apply (Estonian AML Act) |
| Threshold monitoring | Quarterly (internal) | Calculate total AUM; if approaching threshold, notify FI and prepare for full authorization |
| Organizational requirements | Ongoing | Adequate internal controls, conflicts of interest management |
| Marketing restrictions | Per transaction | Can only market to **professional investors** in Estonia; for other EU Member States, must comply with national private placement regimes |

### What You DON'T Need (Sub-Threshold)
- No depositary requirement
- No capital adequacy requirement (EUR 125K base)
- No AIFMD Annex IV reporting
- No remuneration policy
- No AIFMD marketing passport (must use national private placement regimes)
- No leverage limits or transparency requirements
- No formal risk management function separation

## Full-Scope Authorization (If You Cross Thresholds)

### When Required
- Total AUM exceeds EUR 100M (leveraged) or EUR 500M (unleveraged)
- Voluntary opt-in (to access AIFMD marketing passport)
- Required if you want to market to professional investors across EU using the AIFMD passport

### Application Process (3-6 months typical)
1. **Application to Finantsinspektsioon** with comprehensive documentation:
   - Business plan and organizational structure
   - Delegation and oversight arrangements
   - Valuation procedures
   - Liquidity management policy
   - Risk management policy and procedures (including leverage limits)
   - Remuneration policy
   - Conflicts of interest policy
   - Investor complaint handling procedures
   - Depositary agreement
   - Fund documentation (LPA, PPM, articles)

2. **Capital Requirements**:
   - Base: EUR 125,000
   - Additional: 0.02% of AUM exceeding EUR 250M
   - Maximum: EUR 10M
   - Professional indemnity insurance may substitute part of the capital requirement

3. **Depositary Appointment**:
   - Must be an EU credit institution, MiFID investment firm, or other eligible entity
   - Depositary duties: safekeeping of assets, cash flow monitoring, oversight of the AIFM
   - Depositary liability: strict liability for loss of financial instruments
   - Cost: significant — typically 5-15 bps of AUM per annum

4. **Organizational Requirements**:
   - Separation of portfolio management and risk management functions
   - Adequate human and technical resources
   - Senior management responsible for compliance
   - Compliance function (can be outsourced)
   - Internal audit function (for larger managers)
   - Business continuity planning

### AIFMD Marketing Passport
- Authorized AIFMs can market their EU-domiciled AIFs to professional investors across all EU Member States
- Notification procedure: notify home state (Estonia) FI, which notifies host state FI
- Timeline: 20 business days from notification to home state FI
- For non-EU AIFs (e.g., Delaware LP): passport not available; must use national private placement regimes

## Marketing to EU Investors Without a Passport (National Private Placement)

### For Sub-Threshold Managers Marketing Non-EU Funds (Delaware LP)
Each EU Member State has its own private placement regime. Key markets:

**Estonia**: Sub-threshold registered AIFM can market to professional investors in Estonia directly.

**Germany (BaFin)**:
- Notification to BaFin required
- Must appoint a paying agent and information agent in Germany
- German language documentation may be required
- Annual notification fees

**Netherlands (AFM)**:
- Notification to AFM
- Must comply with Dutch AIFM Act (Wft) provisions on marketing
- Dutch language summary may be required

**Luxembourg (CSSF)**:
- Notification to CSSF
- Must have arrangements for paying investors and repurchasing/redeeming units

**UK (FCA)** — Post-Brexit, separate regime:
- FCA notification required
- Must comply with UK marketing restrictions (Financial Promotion Order)
- Reporting obligations

**General Requirements Across Member States**:
1. Cooperation arrangement between home state FI and non-EU fund jurisdiction (Estonia-Delaware: verify MoU exists)
2. Non-EU fund jurisdiction (Delaware) must not be on FATF blacklist ✓
3. DTT between non-EU fund jurisdiction and Member State allowing exchange of tax information
4. The AIFM must comply with AIFMD transparency requirements (annual report, investor disclosure)

## Reporting Requirements

### Sub-Threshold Annual Report (to Finantsinspektsioon)
Required content:
- Main instruments in which the AIFM trades on behalf of the AIFs
- Markets of which it is a member or where it actively trades
- Largest exposures and most important concentrations for each AIF
- Total AUM (to verify threshold status)

### Full-Scope AIFMD Annex IV Reporting
- Detailed quarterly or semi-annual reports (frequency depends on AUM)
- Content: 60+ data fields covering investment strategy, instruments, geography, leverage, risk measures, counterparties, liquidity, stress tests
- XML format via national reporting portal
- Deadlines: 30 days after quarter-end (quarterly filers); 30 days after half-year-end (semi-annual)

### Fund Annual Report (for the AIF itself)
- Must include: balance sheet/statement of assets and liabilities, income and expenditure account, report on activities, material changes, total amount of remuneration paid by AIFM
- Must be audited
- Available to investors within 6 months of financial year end
- Filed with FI within the same period

## Investor Disclosure (Art. 23)

Before investing, provide investors with:
1. Investment strategy and objectives
2. Description of types of assets and use of leverage
3. Description of investment restrictions
4. Circumstances under which the AIF may use leverage, types and sources of leverage, associated risks, maximum level of leverage
5. Procedures for changing investment strategy or policy
6. Main legal implications of the investment (contractual relationship, jurisdiction, applicable law)
7. Identity of AIFM, depositary, auditor, and any other service providers
8. Description of delegated management functions
9. Description of valuation procedures and pricing methodology
10. Description of liquidity risk management, including redemption rights and notice periods
11. All fees, charges, and expenses borne directly or indirectly by investors
12. Fair treatment of investors (any preferential treatment via side letters)
13. Latest annual report
14. NAV per unit/share (or how investors can obtain it)
15. Historical performance of the AIF
16. Prime broker identity and arrangements

## Key Person Considerations Under AIFMD

### Fit and Proper Requirements
- All AIFM directors and senior management must be "fit and proper"
- FI assesses: professional qualifications, knowledge, experience, reputation
- Criminal background checks
- Conflicts of interest disclosure
- For Estonian OÜ board members: additional Commercial Code duties apply (fiduciary duty of care and loyalty)

### Substance Requirements
- AIFM must have adequate substance in Estonia
- Cannot be a letterbox entity — must have actual decision-making capacity
- At least some senior management functions performed from Estonia
- For e-Residency managed companies: FI may scrutinize whether adequate management substance exists in Estonia
- Recommended: At least one board member with regular physical presence in Estonia, or demonstrable digital management infrastructure

## Timeline: From Zero to Fundraising

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1-2 | Form Estonian OÜ (GP + Management Company if separate) |
| 2-3 | Prepare sub-threshold registration notification and supporting docs |
| 3-4 | Submit notification to Finantsinspektsioon |
| 4-6 | FI review and potential questions |
| 5-8 | Simultaneously: form Delaware LP, draft LPA/PPM with Delaware fund counsel |
| 6-8 | FI registration confirmed |
| 8-10 | Finalize fund documents, compliance manual, AML program |
| 10-12 | Begin marketing to prospective LPs |
| 12+ | First close; file Form D with SEC (if US LPs) within 15 days |

**Total estimated timeline: 10-16 weeks from decision to first LP commitment**
