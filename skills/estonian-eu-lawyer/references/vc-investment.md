# VC & Investment Law Reference

## Fund Structures for Cross-Border VC

### Common Structure: Estonian GP + Delaware LP
- **Delaware LP** as the fund vehicle (investor-friendly jurisdiction, well-understood by LPs)
- **Estonian OÜ** as the General Partner (management base)
- **Management Company** (Estonian OÜ or separate entity) providing advisory/management services
- Fund documents: Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA), Subscription Agreement, Side Letters, Management Company SLA

### Why Delaware LP
- DRULPA provides maximum flexibility for LP agreement terms
- No entity-level tax (pass-through)
- Sophisticated body of case law and precedent
- Institutional LPs expect Delaware LP structure
- Can be combined with an Estonian or UAE management entity

### Why Estonian OÜ as GP
- 0% tax on retained profits — carried interest not taxed until distributed
- Low operational costs
- Digital management via e-Residency
- EU passport for fundraising (AIFMD registration)
- Strong rule of law and transparency

### Alternative: Estonian OÜ as Direct Investment Vehicle
- Simpler structure for smaller funds or SPVs
- Use OÜ to hold equity stakes directly
- Shareholder agreement governs investor rights
- 0% CIT on retained earnings = tax-efficient for recycling gains
- Limitation: less familiar to institutional LPs; may face regulatory issues with large numbers of investors

## Venture Partner Agreements

### Structure & Key Terms
- Defines relationship between the fund/GP and a Venture Partner (VP)
- VP is NOT an employee — typically independent contractor or advisory capacity
- VP may receive carry allocation (GP interest) rather than salary

### Critical Provisions
1. **Role Definition**: Executive, fundraising, strategic, operating, or portfolio support — define specifically
2. **Carry Allocation**: Table linking role/commitment level to carry percentage
   - Full-time executive VP: typically 5-15% of GP carry
   - Part-time strategic VP: typically 1-5%
   - Advisory/portfolio support: typically 0.5-2%
3. **Vesting**: Standard 3-4 year vesting with 1-year cliff
   - Monthly ratable vesting after cliff
   - Unvested carry automatically repurchased on termination
   - GP has 90-day window to exercise repurchase right
4. **No Management Fee Rights**: VP acknowledges no entitlement to management fees
5. **Representations**: No competing fund investments, conflict disclosure, no authority to bind fund
6. **Confidentiality**: Strict — access to proprietary deal flow and portfolio data
7. **Termination**: GP can terminate for cause (immediate) or without cause (notice period)
   - Vested carry survives termination
   - Unvested carry forfeited

### Tax Considerations
- Carry treated as capital gains in most jurisdictions (favorable tax treatment)
- Estonian tax: carry distributed from Estonian GP taxed at 22% CIT on distribution
- Delaware tax: carry generally taxed as long-term capital gains (currently 20% + 3.8% NIIT for US persons)
- Cross-border: ensure no double taxation via treaty provisions

## Convertible Instruments

### SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)
- YC-originated instrument; widely used in early-stage funding
- Not a loan — no maturity date, no interest
- Converts to equity on a qualifying financing round, liquidity event, or dissolution
- Key terms: valuation cap, discount rate, MFN clause
- **Estonian context**: SAFEs work under Estonian law but should be carefully drafted to:
  - Comply with Estonian Commercial Code share issuance procedures
  - Address the notarization requirement for share transfers (conversion)
  - Consider pre-emption rights implications on conversion
  - Clarify whether SAFE holder has information rights before conversion

### Convertible Notes
- Loan instrument that converts to equity
- Has maturity date, typically accrues interest
- Key terms: principal, interest rate, maturity, valuation cap, discount, conversion triggers
- **Estonian context**: Regulated as a debt instrument; interest payments subject to withholding tax for non-resident lenders (check DTT rates)

### Estonian Specifics for Convertible Instruments
- Share capital increase requires shareholder resolution (2/3 majority unless articles provide otherwise)
- New shares issuance requires Commercial Register filing
- Pre-emption rights of existing shareholders apply to new share issuances (ÄS § 193) — can be excluded by articles or shareholder resolution
- Conversion mechanics must be carefully mapped to Estonian share issuance procedure

## Shareholder / Investment Agreements

### Standard Provisions for VC Investments
1. **Investment Terms**: Amount, valuation (pre/post-money), share class, price per share
2. **Conditions Precedent**: Due diligence completion, regulatory approvals, board/shareholder approvals
3. **Representations & Warranties**: Standard reps from the company (title, authority, no litigation, financial statements, IP ownership, compliance)
4. **Covenants**: Positive (maintain insurance, comply with laws) and negative (no asset disposals, no new debt without consent)
5. **Board Composition**: Investor director appointment rights
6. **Reserved Matters**: List of decisions requiring investor consent (issuance of shares, M&A, change of business, related party transactions, material contracts, changes to articles)
7. **Information Rights**: Monthly/quarterly financial reports, annual audited accounts, access to books and records
8. **Anti-Dilution**: Full ratchet or weighted average — protects investor against down-rounds
9. **Transfer Restrictions**:
   - ROFR (right of first refusal)
   - Co-sale / tag-along (minority can join a majority sale)
   - Drag-along (majority can force minority to sell)
   - Lock-up period (founders can't sell for X years)
10. **Liquidation Preference**: 1x non-participating is standard; participating preferred gives double-dip
11. **Exit Provisions**: IPO, trade sale, buyback triggers and mechanics
12. **Founder Obligations**: Full-time commitment, non-compete, vesting

### Estonian-Specific Investment Considerations
- Articles of association MUST align with SHA — provisions requiring super-majority or consent in SHA must be reflected in articles to be enforceable against the company
- Share transfer requires notarization or qualified electronic signature
- Dividend preference can be structured via different share classes (requires articles provision)
- Liquidation preference achievable through contractual agreement but should be reflected in articles
- Put/call options: enforceable under Estonian contract law; specific performance available as a remedy

## Fund Formation Document Checklist

1. **Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA)** — governs fund operations, economics, governance
2. **Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)** — offering document for prospective LPs
3. **Subscription Agreement** — LP commitment, representations, KYC/AML
4. **Side Letters** — individual LP accommodations (MFN, co-invest rights, reporting)
5. **Management Company Operating Agreement** — governs management company
6. **Management Company SLA** — allocates responsibilities among managers
7. **Investment Management Agreement** — between fund and management company
8. **Advisory Board / LPAC Terms** — governance body for conflicts
9. **Compliance Manual** — AML/KYC, sanctions, insider trading, personal dealing policies
10. **Regulatory Filings** — AIFMD registration (Estonia), SEC (if US investors), local jurisdiction filings

## Venture Studio Structures

### Common Model
- **Studio Entity** (OÜ or LLC): holds equity in incubated startups
- **Fund Vehicle** (LP or OÜ): provides investment capital
- **Operating Budget**: Separate from investment capital; covers studio costs, salaries, infrastructure
- **Equity Arrangement**: Studio typically takes 20-40% equity for cash + in-kind services
- **Graduation**: Startups that complete the program become eligible for seed/follow-on funding

### Key Legal Documents for Venture Studios
1. **Studio Participation Agreement**: Terms for startups entering the studio (equity, services, IP, milestones)
2. **IP Assignment Agreement**: Startup assigns all IP created during the program to the startup entity (not the studio)
3. **Services Agreement**: Studio provides specific services (mentorship, office space, back-office) in exchange for equity/fees
4. **Co-Investment Agreement**: Terms for studio/fund co-investment in studio companies
5. **Founder Agreement**: Between co-founders of each startup (vesting, roles, IP, non-compete)
