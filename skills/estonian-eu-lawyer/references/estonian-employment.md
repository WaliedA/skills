# Estonian Employment Law Reference

## Primary Legislation
- **Employment Contracts Act** (*Töölepingu seadus*, TLS) — primary employment law statute
- **Occupational Health and Safety Act** (*Töötervishoiu ja tööohutuse seadus*)
- **Equal Treatment Act** (*Võrdse kohtlemise seadus*)

## Employment Contract Fundamentals

### Formation
- Must be in writing (or at minimum employer must provide written summary within 2 weeks)
- Essential terms: parties, start date, job duties, place of work, working time, remuneration
- **Fixed-term**: Only permitted when justified by temporary nature of work; max total 5 years with same employer
- Unjustified use of fixed-term → deemed indefinite-term

### Probation Period
- **Maximum 4 months** (cannot be extended)
- Either party can terminate with **15 calendar days' notice**
- Must be expressly agreed in the contract

### Working Time
- Standard: **8 hours/day, 40 hours/week**
- Maximum including overtime: 48 hours/week averaged over 4-month reference period
- Overtime: only by mutual agreement; compensated at **1.5x rate** or time off (1:1)

### Leave
- **Annual leave**: Minimum **28 calendar days** per year
- **Sick leave**: Employer pays days 4-8 at 70%; Health Insurance Fund from day 9 at 70%
- **Maternity leave**: 140 calendar days (state-compensated)
- **Study leave**: 30 calendar days per year for formal education (20 days paid)

## Termination

### By Employer — Ordinary (Redundancy, Incapacity, Breach)
- Must have valid reason; written notice stating reason
- Notice periods by service length:
  - <1 year: 15 days | 1-5 years: 30 days | 5-10 years: 60 days | 10+: 90 days
- Severance: **1 month's average wage** for redundancy

### By Employer — Extraordinary (Cause)
- No advance notice; serious breach required
- Must give employee opportunity to explain

### By Employee
- **30 calendar days' notice** (no reason required)
- Extraordinary (employer breach): no notice; entitled to 3 months' compensation

### Protected Employees
- Pregnant employees / parental leave: only extraordinary termination
- Employee representatives: enhanced protection
- Filing deadline for challenges: **30 days** from receipt of notice

## Non-Compete Agreements (TLS § 23-24)

**All five conditions must be met:**
1. **In writing**
2. **Maximum 12 months** post-termination
3. **Scope defined**: activities, geography, duration
4. **Reasonable compensation**: Must pay during restriction period (benchmark ~50% of last salary)
5. **Proportionate**: Must protect legitimate business interest

**Critical rules:**
- Worldwide restrictions: only if "particularly vulnerable economic interest" demonstrated
- No compensation = **unenforceable**
- Employer can waive with 30 days' notice
- If employee terminates for employer's breach → non-compete unenforceable
- Contractual penalty permissible but must be reasonable

## NDA / Confidentiality

### During Employment
- Implied duty of confidentiality

### Post-Employment
- Can survive termination **without compensation** (unlike non-compete)
- Information must qualify as genuine trade secret or confidential information
- Cannot be a disguised non-compete
- Typical duration: 2-5 years; indefinite for genuine trade secrets

## IP Created by Employees

### Copyright (Author's Rights Act)
- **Default**: Employer owns economic rights to works created in course of duties
- Moral rights remain with employee (non-transferable under Estonian law)
- Best practice: include express IP assignment clause anyway

### Inventions (Patents Act)
- **Service inventions**: employer can claim ownership within 4 months of notice
- Employee entitled to fair compensation
- **Free inventions**: belong to employee; employer has pre-emptive right if related to employer's field

## Remote Work / Cross-Border
- Estonian law applies if habitual place of work is Estonia (Rome I Regulation, Art. 8)
- Social security: EU coordination rules (Regulation 883/2004)
- Tax residency: 183+ days in Estonia = Estonian tax resident
