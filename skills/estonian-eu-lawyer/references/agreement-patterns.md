# Agreement Patterns & Clause Library

This reference contains common agreement structures and reusable clause patterns based on real-world templates used in Estonian/EU cross-border transactions.

## Partnership Agreement (Tech Provider ↔ Operational Partner)

### Standard Structure
1. **Preamble**: Effective date, parties (with jurisdiction, address, representative)
2. **Recitals**: Purpose and context of the agreement
3. **Subject of Agreement**: What the partnership covers; exclusivity scope
4. **Territory**: Geographic scope; exclusive vs. non-exclusive rights
5. **Duties of Parties**: Detailed obligations for each party
6. **Business Model**: Revenue sharing, pricing, payment terms
7. **IP & Ownership**: Who owns what (platform IP vs. brand vs. customer data)
8. **Legal Responsibilities**: Liability allocation
9. **Term**: Duration, auto-renewal, termination notice
10. **Governing Law**: Applicable jurisdiction
11. **Confidentiality**: Mutual obligation
12. **Signatures**: With appendices (growth plans, KPIs)

### Key Clauses

**Exclusivity with Performance Thresholds**:
```
Operational Partner has an exclusive right to provide [SERVICE] in [TERRITORY].
If Operational Partner fails to meet the performance targets set forth in
Appendix [X], Technical Partner may revoke the exclusive right upon [30] days'
written notice, converting the arrangement to a non-exclusive basis.
```

**IP Retention (Provider-Friendly)**:
```
Customer/Operational Partner does not acquire any ownership or intellectual
property rights in any part of the Platform, including branded applications
and features developed pursuant to Customer's customization requests.
Provider retains full ownership of the Platform, applications, and all
derivative works.
```

**Customer Data Ownership**:
```
Customer is the sole and rightful owner of all end-user data (passengers,
drivers, customers) registered under the "[BRAND]" brand. Provider shall
not use, share, or monetize Customer's end-user data except as necessary
to provide the Platform services. Upon termination, Provider shall export
and deliver all Customer data in a standard machine-readable format within
[30] days.
```

**Liability Separation (Platform Provider)**:
```
Provider is the technical provider of the [PLATFORM] solution and shall in
no way be bound by or responsible for the [services] provided by [end-users]
registered by Operational Partner. Operational Partner assumes full
responsibility for services provided through the Platform in the Territory.
```

## Venture Partner Agreement

### Standard Structure
1. **Parties Section**: Fund, GP, Management Company, Venture Partner
2. **Share/Unit Terms**: Class, number, price, vesting commencement, cliff, vesting period
3. **Venture Partner Role**: Checklist of responsibilities (Executive, Fundraising, Strategic, Operating, Portfolio)
4. **Compensation Guidelines**: Carry allocation table by role and commitment level
5. **Representations & Covenants**: No competing investments, conflict disclosure, no binding authority, acknowledgment of no management fee rights
6. **General Terms**: Engagement, compensation, purchase terms (vesting mechanics), confidentiality, indemnification, termination, general provisions
7. **Signature Page**: VP, VP Entity (if applicable), GP

### Critical Vesting Clause Pattern
```
The Shares shall vest ratably monthly from the Vesting Commencement Date for
[NUMBER] years, provided that no Shares shall vest until Venture Partner has
provided services for [NUMBER] months from the Vesting Commencement Date
(the "Cliff Date"). Upon the Cliff Date, Venture Partner shall vest in all
Shares corresponding to the cliff period. Upon termination, General Partner
automatically repurchases all unvested Shares at the original purchase price
per Share. General Partner must notify Venture Partner in writing within
ninety (90) days if it elects NOT to exercise its repurchase right.
```

## Employment / Job Offer Letter

### Standard Structure (Multi-Jurisdiction Aware)
1. **Header**: Company name, address, date
2. **Position Details**: Title, location, start date, reporting line
3. **Job Description**: Key responsibilities (bullet points)
4. **Compensation**: Base salary, allowances (position, communication, transportation), tax treatment per local law
5. **Working Hours**: Standard hours, days per week
6. **Leave**: Annual leave, sick leave, public holidays (per local labor law)
7. **Probation**: Duration per local law requirements
8. **Confidentiality**: Reference to separate NDA
9. **Acceptance**: Signature block with deadline

### Jurisdiction-Specific Adjustments
- **Estonia**: Probation max 4 months; 28 days annual leave; reference TLS
- **UAE**: Probation max 6 months; 30 days annual leave; reference MOHRE regulations
- **Ethiopia**: Probation per Ethiopian labor code; reference specific ETB amounts; tax per Ethiopian law

## Service Level Agreement (Management Company)

### Standard Structure
1. **Definitions**: Fund, GP, Management Company, Managers, Effective Date
2. **Parties and Purpose**: Accountability and alignment statement
3. **Scope of Responsibilities**: Table format allocating duties to specific managers
4. **Amendments**: Written consent required
5. **Disengagement**: Consequences for non-performance (immediate or with probation)
6. **Signatures**: With time commitment indication (full-time/part-time, hours/week)

## General Memorandum of Agreement

### Standard Structure
1. **Parties**: Names, addresses, defined terms
2. **Purpose and Scope**: Collaboration objective, project description
3. **Covenants, Representations & Warranties**: Capability confirmation
4. **General Provisions**: Termination rights, entire agreement
5. **Applicable Law**: Governing jurisdiction
6. **Signatures**

## Business Funding Agreement

### Standard Structure
1. **Parties**: Funding Entity, Recipient Business (with jurisdiction details)
2. **Nature of Funding**: Grant/loan/equity — must be unambiguous
3. **Term and Termination**: Duration, early termination grounds (insolvency, business discontinuation, material breach)
4. **Funding Procedures**: Amount, disbursement schedule, payment method
5. **Interest, Fees, Finance Charges**: Rate, calculation, additional charges
6. **Repayment Procedures**: Schedule, installment amounts, payment method
7. **Documentation and Inspection**: Audit rights
8. **Confidentiality**: Mutual
9. **Compliance with Law**: Both parties
10. **Relationship of Parties**: Independent contractors
11. **Assignment**: No transfer without written consent
12. **Governing Law and Jurisdiction**

## Universal Boilerplate Clauses

### Governing Law (Estonian)
```
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
the laws of the Republic of Estonia, without regard to its conflict
of laws provisions.
```

### Arbitration (Estonian Chamber of Commerce)
```
Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be
finally resolved by arbitration under the Rules of the Court of Arbitration
of the Estonian Chamber of Commerce and Industry. The tribunal shall consist
of [one/three] arbitrator(s). The seat of arbitration shall be Tallinn,
Estonia. The language of the proceedings shall be English.
```

### Entire Agreement
```
This Agreement, together with all schedules, appendices, and exhibits
attached hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties
with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior
negotiations, representations, warranties, commitments, offers,
contracts, and agreements, whether written or oral.
```

### Severability
```
If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or
unenforceable, the validity, legality, and enforceability of the
remaining provisions shall not in any way be affected or impaired,
and such provision shall be reformed to the minimum extent necessary
to make it valid, legal, and enforceable while preserving the Parties'
original intent.
```

### Force Majeure
```
Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing
its obligations under this Agreement where such failure or delay results
from Force Majeure, meaning any event beyond the reasonable control of
the affected Party, including but not limited to: natural disasters,
pandemic, war, terrorism, government sanctions, strikes, or failure of
third-party telecommunications or power supply. The affected Party shall
notify the other Party promptly and use reasonable efforts to mitigate
the impact. If Force Majeure continues for more than [90] days, either
Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice.
```

### Notices
```
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed
duly given when: (a) delivered personally; (b) sent by certified mail,
return receipt requested; (c) sent by internationally recognized courier
service; or (d) sent by email to the addresses set forth herein, provided
that a confirmation copy is sent by one of methods (a)-(c) within [3]
business days. Email notice alone shall be sufficient for routine
communications but not for notices of termination, breach, or dispute.
```
