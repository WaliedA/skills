## Certificate of Incorporation (C-Corp) {#cert-incorp}

Filed with the Delaware Secretary of State under DGCL § 101–102. This is the charter document for a Delaware corporation.

### Template

```
CERTIFICATE OF INCORPORATION
OF
[COMPANY NAME], INC.

FIRST: The name of the corporation is [Company Name], Inc. (the "Corporation").

SECOND: The registered office of the Corporation in the State of Delaware is
located at [Address], in the City of [City], County of [County]. The name of
its registered agent at such address is [Registered Agent Name].

THIRD: The purpose of the Corporation is to engage in any lawful act or activity
for which corporations may be organized under the General Corporation Law of
the State of Delaware.

FOURTH: The total number of shares of stock which the Corporation shall have
authority to issue is [●] shares, consisting of:

  (a) [●] shares of Common Stock, par value $[0.0001/0.001/0.01] per share
      ("Common Stock"); and

  (b) [●] shares of Preferred Stock, par value $[0.0001/0.001/0.01] per share
      ("Preferred Stock").

The Board of Directors is hereby authorized to provide for the issuance of
Preferred Stock in one or more series and to fix the designations, powers,
preferences, rights, qualifications, limitations, and restrictions thereof.

FIFTH: The name and mailing address of the incorporator is:
[Name]
[Address]

SIXTH: The following provisions are included for the management of the business
and the conduct of the affairs of the Corporation:

  (a) The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by or under
      the direction of the Board of Directors. The number of directors shall be
      fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board of Directors.

  (b) The Board of Directors may adopt, amend, or repeal the bylaws of the
      Corporation.

SEVENTH: To the fullest extent permitted by the General Corporation Law of the
State of Delaware, as the same exists or may hereafter be amended, a director
of the Corporation shall not be personally liable to the Corporation or its
stockholders for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty as a director.

[OPTIONAL — Officer Exculpation per 2022 DGCL Amendment:]
To the fullest extent permitted by the General Corporation Law of the State of
Delaware, as the same exists or may hereafter be amended, an officer of the
Corporation shall not be personally liable to the Corporation or its stockholders
for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty as an officer.

EIGHTH: The Corporation shall indemnify and hold harmless, to the fullest extent
permitted by applicable law as it presently exists or may hereafter be amended,
any person who was or is made or is threatened to be made a party to or is
otherwise involved in any action, suit, or proceeding by reason of the fact that
such person is or was a director or officer of the Corporation.

NINTH: Unless the Corporation consents in writing to the selection of an
alternative forum, the Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware shall be the
sole and exclusive forum for: (i) any derivative action brought on behalf of the
Corporation; (ii) any action asserting a claim of breach of fiduciary duty;
(iii) any action arising pursuant to the DGCL; or (iv) any action asserting a
claim governed by the internal affairs doctrine.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned, being the incorporator hereinbefore named,
has executed this Certificate of Incorporation as of [Date].

___________________________
[Incorporator Name], Incorporator
```

### Practitioner Notes

**Authorized shares:** For early-stage startups, 10,000,000 shares of Common Stock is standard. Blocker corps for fund structures typically need minimal authorized shares.

**Par value:** $0.0001 is most common for startups (minimizes franchise tax under the authorized shares method). For blocker corps, par value matters less.

**Preferred Stock blank check:** The "blank check" preferred authorization lets the board create series of preferred without stockholder approval. Essential for venture-backed companies. For fund blocker corps, usually unnecessary.

**Forum selection (NINTH):** Post-*Salzberg v. Sciabacucchi*, Delaware courts have upheld exclusive forum provisions. Market standard for all new incorporations.

**Filing fee:** $89 base + franchise tax calculation. Expedited service available (24-hour: $100; same-day: $500; 2-hour: $1,000).

## Certificate of Formation (LLC) {#cert-formation-llc}

Filed under DLLCA § 18-201. Minimal filing.

### Template

```
CERTIFICATE OF FORMATION
OF
[ENTITY NAME], LLC

1. The name of the limited liability company is [Entity Name], LLC
   (the "Company").

2. The address of the registered office of the Company in the State of
   Delaware is [Address], City of [City], County of [County], Delaware
   [Zip Code].

3. The name and address of the registered agent for service of process on
   the Company in the State of Delaware is [Registered Agent Name],
   [Address].

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned authorized person has executed this
Certificate of Formation as of [Date].

___________________________
Name: [●]
Title: Authorized Person
```

### Practitioner Notes

**Simplicity:** Unlike a certificate of incorporation, the certificate of formation requires almost nothing. All substantive governance lives in the LLC Agreement.

**Authorized person:** Any person authorized by the members may sign. Does not need to be a member or manager.

**Filing fee:** $90. Expedited options same as corporations.

**GP entities:** When forming the GP LLC for a fund, file the Certificate of Formation before executing the LP Agreement (the LP needs a validly formed GP).

## Certificate of Limited Partnership {#cert-lp}

Filed under DRULPA § 17-201.

### Template

```
CERTIFICATE OF LIMITED PARTNERSHIP
OF
[FUND NAME], L.P.

1. The name of the limited partnership is [Fund Name], L.P.
   (the "Partnership").

2. The address of the registered office of the Partnership in the State of
   Delaware is [Address], City of [City], County of [County], Delaware
   [Zip Code].

3. The name and address of the registered agent for service of process on
   the Partnership in the State of Delaware is [Registered Agent Name],
   [Address].

4. The name and business address of the sole general partner of the
   Partnership is:

   [GP Entity Name], LLC
   [Business Address]

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned general partner has executed this
Certificate of Limited Partnership as of [Date].

[GP ENTITY NAME], LLC
General Partner

By: ___________________________
Name: [●]
Title: [Managing Member / Authorized Person]
```

### Practitioner Notes

**Formation sequence:** Form the GP LLC first (Certificate of Formation), then file the Certificate of Limited Partnership naming the GP. Execute the LP Agreement after both filings.

**Filing fee:** $200.

**Annual tax:** $300/year. Due June 1.

**Amendment:** Required only for changes to the GP or registered agent. No amendment needed for LP admissions, capital changes, or LPA amendments.

## Filing Practicalities {#filing}

### Delaware Secretary of State

**Online filing:** https://icis.corp.delaware.gov/ecorp/

**Common registered agents:**
- Corporation Service Company (CSC)
- CT Corporation (Wolters Kluwer)
- National Registered Agents, Inc. (NRAI)
- Registered Agents Inc. (budget option)
- Incorp Services

**Typical registered agent fees:** $50–$300/year depending on provider and services.

### Formation Sequence for a Fund

Standard order of operations:

1. **Form GP LLC** — File Certificate of Formation ($90). Execute LLC Agreement.
2. **Form Fund LP** — File Certificate of Limited Partnership naming GP ($200). Execute LP Agreement.
3. **Form Management Company** (if separate from GP) — File Certificate of Formation ($90). Execute LLC Agreement.
4. **Obtain EIN** — IRS Form SS-4 for each entity. Can be done online immediately.
5. **Open bank accounts** — Fund LP account, GP account, management company account.
6. **Register with state of operations** — If operating outside Delaware (e.g., UAE, California), register as a foreign entity in that jurisdiction.
7. **File Form D** — Within 15 days of first sale of securities (LP interests). File with SEC via EDGAR.

### Annual Maintenance

| Entity | Annual Tax | Due Date | Filing |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------|
| LP | $300 | June 1 | Annual tax payment only |
| LLC | $300 | June 1 | Annual tax payment only |
| Corporation | Varies ($400 min) | March 1 | Annual franchise tax report + payment |

**Late penalties:** $200 penalty + 1.5% monthly interest. Entities can be voided/forfeited for non-payment.
