## DRULPA — Delaware Revised Uniform Limited Partnership Act {#drulpa}

Title 6, Chapter 17, Delaware Code.

### Freedom of Contract (§ 17-1101)

The cornerstone of Delaware LP law. Section 17-1101(c) provides that the LP agreement governs relations among partners and between partners and the partnership. The policy of DRULPA is "to give the maximum effect to the principle of freedom of contract and to the enforceability of partnership agreements."

**Practical impact:** Nearly every default rule in DRULPA can be overridden by the LP agreement. This makes the LP agreement — not the statute — the primary governing document for Delaware fund LPs.

### Limited Partner Liability Shield (§ 17-303)

An LP is not liable for the obligations of the LP solely by reason of being an LP, even if the LP participates in management. Section 17-303(b) lists safe harbor activities that do not constitute "participating in control," including:
- Being a contractor, agent, or employee of the LP or GP
- Consulting with the GP on LP business
- Acting as surety or guarantor
- Voting on amendments, dissolution, sale of assets, admission of partners
- Serving on an LPAC or advisory committee

**Practical impact:** LPs in fund structures can serve on LPACs, approve key decisions, and have consent rights without risking liability. This safe harbor is why Delaware LPs are the dominant fund vehicle.

### Fiduciary Duties (§ 17-1101(d))

The LP agreement may:
- Expand or restrict fiduciary duties (including eliminating them entirely)
- Expand or restrict the implied contractual covenant of good faith and fair dealing (but cannot eliminate it)

**Practical impact:** Most fund LPAs modify fiduciary duties significantly — replacing common law fiduciary standards with contractual standards (e.g., GP liable only for "bad faith, willful misconduct, or gross negligence"). This is standard market practice. The implied covenant of good faith is the irreducible floor.

### Series LPs (§ 17-218)

An LP agreement may establish designated series of LP interests with:
- Separate rights, powers, and duties
- Separate assets and liabilities (internal shields between series)
- Separate business purposes

**Practical impact:** Used for multi-strategy funds, parallel funds, and alternative investment vehicles. Each series can have different investors and economics. The internal liability shield between series has not been extensively tested in litigation — some practitioners still prefer separate LPs for clean bankruptcy remoteness.

### Dissolution (§ 17-801)

Default dissolution triggers include:
- Time specified in certificate/LP agreement
- Events specified in LP agreement
- Written consent of all partners
- Withdrawal of a GP (unless agreement provides otherwise or remaining GPs continue)
- Judicial dissolution

**Practical impact:** Fund LPAs should always specify the fund term and extension mechanics, and provide that GP removal/withdrawal does not automatically dissolve the fund.

### Merger, Conversion, Domestication (§ 17-211, 217, 215)

Delaware LPs can:
- Merge with other DE or non-DE entities (§ 17-211)
- Convert to a different entity type (§ 17-217)
- Domesticate from a non-DE entity (§ 17-215)

**Practical impact:** Useful for fund restructurings, GP-led secondaries, and continuation fund transactions. Conversion/merger mechanics must be authorized in the LP agreement or by the vote specified therein.

## DLLCA — Delaware Limited Liability Company Act {#dllca}

Title 6, Chapter 18, Delaware Code.

### Freedom of Contract (§ 18-1101(b))

Same policy as DRULPA: "maximum effect to the principle of freedom of contract and to the enforceability of limited liability company agreements."

**Practical impact:** The LLC agreement is king. Delaware LLCs used as fund GPs and management companies can be structured with virtually any governance, economics, and duty regime the parties agree to.

### Fiduciary Duty Modification (§ 18-1101(c)–(e))

- LLC agreements may restrict or eliminate fiduciary duties
- Cannot eliminate the implied contractual covenant of good faith and fair dealing
- Members/managers who rely in good faith on the LLC agreement are protected

**Practical impact:** GP entity LLC agreements commonly eliminate fiduciary duties and replace them with a contractual "bad acts" standard, mirroring the fund LPA.

### Distributions (§ 18-607)

A member is liable to the LLC for distributions received if, at the time of distribution, the LLC's liabilities (excluding liabilities to members on account of their interests) exceed fair value of assets. Three-year lookback.

**Practical impact:** Relevant for management fee and carry distributions from GP/management company entities. Members who receive wrongful distributions may have to return them.

### Series LLCs (§ 18-215)

Similar to Series LPs — separate series with segregated assets, liabilities, members, and managers. Each series can have its own purpose and economics.

**Practical impact:** Used for multi-fund management platforms, SPV structures, and fund-of-funds. Same caveat on untested internal shields.

### Division (§ 18-217)

A Delaware LLC can divide into two or more resulting LLCs, with assets and liabilities allocated among them per a plan of division.

**Practical impact:** Relatively new (2018). Useful for fund platform restructurings, spinning off fund management businesses, or creating clean separations between business lines.

## DGCL — Delaware General Corporation Law {#dgcl}

Title 8, Delaware Code.

### Certificate of Incorporation (§ 102)

Required contents: name, address, agent, purpose, authorized shares, incorporator. Optional provisions under § 102(b) allow significant customization.

### Exculpation (§ 102(b)(7))

Certificate may eliminate or limit personal liability of directors for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty of care. Cannot exculpate for:
- Breach of duty of loyalty
- Acts not in good faith or involving intentional misconduct
- Unlawful distributions (§ 174)
- Improper personal benefit

**2022 Amendment:** Extended exculpation to certain officers (senior executive officers listed in SEC filings) for direct claims. Does not cover derivative claims against officers.

**Practical impact:** Standard in all Delaware corporate charters. For fund blocker corps, this is routine. The officer exculpation amendment (effective Aug 1, 2022) is a significant development.

### Fiduciary Duties — Key Doctrines

Unlike LPs and LLCs, Delaware corporate fiduciary duties cannot be eliminated. Key standards:

- **Business Judgment Rule:** Presumption that directors acted on an informed basis, in good faith, and in honest belief the action was in the corporation's best interest. Plaintiff must rebut.
- **Entire Fairness:** Applies when board has a conflict (self-dealing, controlling stockholder transaction). Must show fair dealing AND fair price.
- **Revlon Duties:** When a sale or change of control is inevitable, board must maximize stockholder value.
- **Unocal/Unitrin:** Defensive measures against hostile takeovers must be reasonable in relation to the threat.

**Practical impact:** For fund blocker corps and GP entities organized as corporations (rare but sometimes done for tax or regulatory reasons), fiduciary duties are more rigid than for LPs/LLCs. This is one reason LLCs are preferred for GP entities.

## Cross-Cutting Themes {#cross-cutting}

### Freedom of Contract Hierarchy
- **Corporations:** Least flexible — fiduciary duties are mandatory; charter and bylaws must comply with DGCL
- **LLCs:** Most flexible — essentially any deal the parties agree to, subject to implied covenant
- **LPs:** Very flexible — similar to LLCs; LP agreement governs almost everything

### Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing
The irreducible minimum across all Delaware entity types. Cannot be eliminated in LP or LLC agreements. It's a gap-filler — it doesn't override express contract terms but prevents parties from acting in bad faith in areas the agreement doesn't address.

Key case law: *Gerber v. Enterprise Products Holdings* (Del. 2013), *Nemec v. Shrader* (Del. 2010).

### Registered Agent Requirement
All Delaware entities must maintain a registered agent in Delaware. Failure to maintain an agent can result in forfeiture of good standing. Common agents: Corporation Service Company (CSC), CT Corporation, National Registered Agents.

### Annual Franchise Tax
- LPs: $300/year flat fee
- LLCs: $300/year flat fee
- Corporations: Calculated by authorized shares method or assumed par value capital method (take the lower); minimum $400, maximum $200,000+

**Practical impact:** For fund structures with multiple entities, the aggregate franchise tax adds up. Factor this into fund formation budgets.
