# Venture Partner Agreement Reference

## Overview & When to Use {#overview}

A Venture Partner Agreement governs the relationship between the fund's GP/management company and an individual who contributes to the fund on a part-time or deal-specific basis — typically sourcing deals, conducting diligence, supporting portfolio companies, or raising capital — without being a full-time partner or employee.

**When to use:** Onboarding an individual who will:
- Source and introduce deal flow
- Assist with fundraising / LP introductions
- Provide sector expertise or portfolio support
- Participate in fund economics (carry and/or management fee share) tied to their contributions

**Key distinction from full GP partners:** Venture Partners typically:
- Do not have management authority over the fund
- Do not sit on the GP's investment committee (unless specifically granted)
- Have carry/fee participation that is performance-contingent rather than guaranteed
- May work part-time or maintain other professional activities

## Venture Partner Agreement Template {#template}

```
VENTURE PARTNER AGREEMENT

This Venture Partner Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of
[Effective Date], by and between:

[GP Entity Name], LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (the "General
Partner"), acting in its capacity as the general partner of [Fund Name], L.P.,
a Delaware limited partnership (the "Fund");

and

[Venture Partner Name], an individual residing at [Address]
(the "Venture Partner").

The General Partner and the Venture Partner are each referred to herein as a
"Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the General Partner serves as the general partner of the Fund, a
venture capital fund focused on [investment strategy / geographic focus /
stage focus];

WHEREAS, the Venture Partner possesses expertise, relationships, and
capabilities that the General Partner believes will be valuable to the Fund's
investment activities and fundraising efforts;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to set forth the terms under which the Venture
Partner will provide services to the Fund and participate in the Fund's
economics;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements
herein, the Parties agree as follows:

SECTION 1. DEFINITIONS

"Capital Raised" means the aggregate capital commitments obtained from Limited
Partners that are directly attributable to introductions or fundraising efforts
by the Venture Partner, as reasonably determined by the General Partner.

"Carried Interest" means the share of net profits of the Fund allocated to the
General Partner pursuant to the Fund's Agreement of Limited Partnership (the
"LPA").

"Carried Interest Participation" has the meaning set forth in Section 4(a).

"Fund" means [Fund Name], L.P.

"Investment Period" means the period during which the Fund is authorized to
make new investments, as defined in the LPA.

"Management Fee" means the management fee payable to the General Partner (or
its designee) pursuant to the LPA.

"Management Fee Participation" has the meaning set forth in Section 4(b).

"Sourced Deal" means an investment made by the Fund in a portfolio company that
was introduced to the Fund by the Venture Partner, as reasonably determined by
the General Partner.

"Term" has the meaning set forth in Section 6.

SECTION 2. ROLE AND RESPONSIBILITIES

(a) Services. The Venture Partner shall provide the following services to the
    Fund and the General Partner (collectively, the "Services"):

    (i)   Identify, introduce, and help evaluate potential investment
          opportunities consistent with the Fund's investment strategy;

    (ii)  Assist with fundraising efforts, including introductions to
          prospective Limited Partners and participation in investor meetings
          and due diligence processes;

    (iii) Provide portfolio support to Fund portfolio companies as reasonably
          requested by the General Partner, including mentorship, strategic
          advice, and introductions;

    (iv)  Participate in investment committee discussions when invited by the
          General Partner (for advisory purposes; the Venture Partner shall
          not have a vote on the investment committee unless separately agreed
          in writing); and

    (v)   Such other services as mutually agreed by the Parties from time
          to time.

(b) Time Commitment. The Venture Partner is not required to devote full-time
    efforts to the Fund. The Venture Partner may engage in other business
    activities, provided that such activities do not (i) create a material
    conflict of interest with the Fund or (ii) violate the non-compete
    provisions of Section 5.

(c) Independent Contractor. The Venture Partner is an independent contractor
    and not an employee, partner, or agent of the General Partner or the Fund.
    Nothing in this Agreement shall create an employment, partnership, joint
    venture, or agency relationship between the Parties.

SECTION 3. EXPENSES

(a) The General Partner shall reimburse the Venture Partner for reasonable,
    pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the
    Services, including travel expenses for Fund-related meetings, conferences,
    and LP/portfolio company site visits.

(b) Expense reimbursement requests shall be submitted with supporting
    documentation within [30/60] days of being incurred.

(c) Expenses exceeding $[●] individually or $[●] in aggregate per
    [quarter/year] shall require prior written approval of the General Partner.

SECTION 4. ECONOMICS

(a) Carried Interest Participation. The Venture Partner shall be entitled to
    receive [●]% of the Carried Interest received by the General Partner from
    the Fund (the "Carried Interest Participation"), subject to the following:

    (i)   The Carried Interest Participation shall vest [ratably over [●]
          years from the Effective Date / based on performance milestones as
          set forth in Exhibit A / immediately upon execution of this
          Agreement];

    (ii)  The Carried Interest Participation shall be calculated after giving
          effect to any clawback obligations of the General Partner under the
          LPA;

    (iii) The Carried Interest Participation relates solely to the Fund and
          does not extend to any successor fund or parallel vehicle unless
          separately agreed in writing;

    (iv)  Payment of the Carried Interest Participation shall be made within
          [30] days following the General Partner's receipt of Carried Interest
          distributions from the Fund.

(b) Management Fee Participation. [OPTION A — Fundraising-Linked:]

    The Venture Partner shall be entitled to receive [●]% of the Management
    Fee attributable to Capital Raised by the Venture Partner (the "Management
    Fee Participation"). For the avoidance of doubt, if the Venture Partner
    is directly responsible for $[●] in Capital Raised, the Management Fee
    Participation shall equal [●]% of the Management Fee calculated on such
    Capital Raised amount.

    [OPTION B — Fixed Percentage:]

    The Venture Partner shall be entitled to receive [●]% of the aggregate
    Management Fee received by the General Partner from the Fund.

    [OPTION C — None:]

    The Venture Partner shall not participate in the Management Fee.

(c) Performance Milestones. [OPTIONAL — Include if carry/fee participation
    is performance-contingent:]

    The Carried Interest Participation and/or Management Fee Participation
    shall be subject to the performance milestones set forth in Exhibit A.
    The General Partner shall determine, in its reasonable discretion, whether
    each milestone has been achieved.

(d) Tax Treatment. Each Party shall be responsible for its own tax obligations
    arising from payments under this Agreement. The Venture Partner
    acknowledges that the Carried Interest Participation may be subject to
    Section 1061 of the Internal Revenue Code (3-year holding period for
    long-term capital gains treatment). The General Partner shall provide
    the Venture Partner with such tax information as is reasonably necessary
    for the Venture Partner to prepare its tax returns.

SECTION 5. RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS

(a) Non-Compete. During the Term and for a period of [●] months following
    termination, the Venture Partner shall not, directly or indirectly:

    (i)   Serve as a general partner, managing member, investment advisor, or
          in a similar capacity for any pooled investment vehicle with an
          investment strategy substantially similar to the Fund's strategy in
          the Fund's target geography; or

    (ii)  Solicit any Limited Partner of the Fund to invest in a competing
          fund.

    Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Venture Partner may (A) make personal
    angel investments in amounts not exceeding $[●] per investment, (B)
    serve as an advisor or board member to companies outside the Fund's
    target sector, and (C) continue any pre-existing business activities
    disclosed to the General Partner prior to the Effective Date.

(b) Non-Solicitation. During the Term and for [●] months thereafter, the
    Venture Partner shall not solicit or recruit any employee, consultant, or
    other venture partner of the General Partner or its affiliates.

(c) Confidentiality. The Venture Partner shall maintain the confidentiality
    of all non-public information regarding the Fund, its portfolio companies,
    its Limited Partners, and the General Partner's proprietary processes,
    including deal flow, investment theses, LP identities and commitments,
    and fund performance data. This obligation survives termination
    indefinitely.

(d) Intellectual Property. Any investment theses, research, deal memos, or
    other work product created by the Venture Partner in connection with the
    Services shall be the property of the General Partner.

SECTION 6. TERM AND TERMINATION

(a) Term. This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and
    shall continue until the earlier of: (i) the dissolution of the Fund;
    (ii) mutual written agreement of the Parties; or (iii) termination by
    either Party pursuant to Section 6(b) or 6(c) (the "Term").

(b) Termination by General Partner. The General Partner may terminate this
    Agreement:

    (i)   For Cause, immediately upon written notice. "Cause" means: (A)
          material breach of this Agreement that is not cured within [30]
          days of written notice; (B) fraud, willful misconduct, or gross
          negligence; (C) conviction of a felony or crime involving moral
          turpitude; or (D) material violation of applicable securities laws.

    (ii)  Without Cause, upon [60/90] days' prior written notice.

(c) Termination by Venture Partner. The Venture Partner may terminate this
    Agreement upon [60/90] days' prior written notice to the General Partner.

(d) Effect of Termination.

    (i)   If terminated for Cause: The Venture Partner shall forfeit all
          unvested Carried Interest Participation and all unvested Management
          Fee Participation. Vested amounts shall remain payable.

    (ii)  If terminated without Cause (by either Party): All Carried Interest
          Participation shall [fully vest / vest on an accelerated basis
          through the next vesting date / remain subject to the original
          vesting schedule]. Management Fee Participation shall cease as of
          the termination date [or continue for [●] months following
          termination].

    (iii) Confidentiality, non-compete, non-solicitation, and intellectual
          property obligations shall survive termination as specified herein.

SECTION 7. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

Each Party represents and warrants that: (a) it has the authority and capacity
to enter into this Agreement; (b) the execution and performance of this
Agreement does not conflict with any other agreement to which it is a party;
and (c) it will comply with all applicable laws in performing its obligations
hereunder.

SECTION 8. GENERAL PROVISIONS

(a) Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in
    accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to
    conflicts of law principles.

(b) Dispute Resolution. Any dispute arising out of this Agreement shall be
    resolved by [binding arbitration under the rules of [AAA/JAMS] in
    [City, State] / the Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware].

(c) Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between
    the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions and agreements.

(d) Amendment. This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument
    signed by both Parties.

(e) Assignment. The Venture Partner may not assign this Agreement without
    the prior written consent of the General Partner. The General Partner
    may assign this Agreement to any successor general partner of the Fund.

(f) Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses
    set forth above (or such other address as a Party may designate).

(g) Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of
    which shall be an original.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Parties have executed this Agreement as of the date
first written above.

[GP ENTITY NAME], LLC

By: ___________________________
Name: [●]
Title: [Managing Member]

VENTURE PARTNER:

___________________________
[Venture Partner Name]
```

## Key Negotiation Points {#negotiation}

### Carry Allocation
The VP's carry is typically carved out of the GP's 20% carry — not additional carry on top. So if the VP gets 5% of carry, the GP principals' share decreases from 20% to 15% of fund profits. For emerging managers, every percentage point matters.

**Range:** 1–10% of carry for Venture Partners, depending on contribution level. Deal-specific carry (carry only on Sourced Deals) is an alternative to fund-wide carry.

### Management Fee Participation
Two common models:
- **Fundraising-linked:** VP gets a percentage of management fees attributable to capital they raised. Directly incentivizes fundraising. Common for emerging managers.
- **Fixed percentage:** VP gets a flat share of total management fees. Simpler but less performance-aligned.

⚠️ **Risk:** Management fee sharing can create regulatory issues if the VP is effectively acting as a placement agent. If the VP's primary role is fundraising, consider whether broker-dealer registration is required.

### Attribution Disputes
"Who sourced the deal" and "who raised the capital" can be contentious. The agreement should specify that the GP determines attribution in its reasonable discretion, and that the GP's determination is final absent manifest error.

### Vesting
Carry vesting protects the GP if the VP leaves early. Common structures:
- 3–4 year ratable vesting (25–33% per year)
- Milestone-based vesting (tied to capital raised or deals sourced)
- Cliff + ratable (1-year cliff, then monthly/quarterly vesting)
- Immediate vesting (less common; used for senior/established VPs)

### Post-Termination Economics
Critical to define clearly. Questions to resolve:
- Does unvested carry accelerate on termination without cause?
- Does the VP continue to receive carry on deals sourced during the term?
- Does management fee participation continue post-termination?
- What happens to carry if the VP joins a competing fund?

## Structuring Considerations {#structuring}

### Tax Implications
- Carry allocated to VPs is subject to the same § 1061 3-year holding period rules
- VP carry should be structured as a profits interest in the GP entity (not as compensation) to preserve capital gains treatment
- The VP should receive a K-1 from the GP entity, not a 1099
- Consult tax counsel on whether the VP's arrangement constitutes a "service partner" interest requiring § 83 analysis

### Regulatory Considerations
- If the VP is primarily fundraising, broker-dealer exemption analysis is required
- If the VP has investment discretion, investment adviser registration considerations apply
- The VP should not be held out as a "partner" of the fund to LPs unless they are actually a member of the GP entity

### GP Entity Membership
Some structures bring the VP into the GP LLC as a member (with a defined carry allocation in the GP Operating Agreement) rather than using a separate Venture Partner Agreement. Trade-offs:
- **Separate agreement:** Simpler, more flexibility to modify, clearer separation
- **GP membership:** Stronger alignment, may have better tax treatment, but harder to unwind

For emerging managers, a separate Venture Partner Agreement is typically preferred for flexibility.

## Common Variations {#variations}

### Deal-Specific Carry
VP receives carry only on deals they sourced, not fund-wide. Calculated on a deal-by-deal basis. More common for part-time VPs or operating advisors.

### Advisory Board VP
VP serves primarily on the LPAC or as a strategic advisor. Minimal carry (1–2%), no management fee participation. More of an honorary/advisory role.

### Fundraising-Only VP
VP's sole role is LP introductions and fundraising support. Economics tied entirely to Capital Raised. No deal sourcing component. Higher regulatory risk (broker-dealer analysis needed).

### Operating VP
VP provides hands-on portfolio support (e.g., CTO-in-residence, CFO advisory). May receive carry plus consulting fees from portfolio companies (with appropriate conflict disclosure).
