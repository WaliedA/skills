## General Engagement Letter Principles {#general}

Engagement letters define the scope, fees, responsibilities, and limitations of a professional relationship. For fund structures, they serve several purposes:

- **Scope definition:** What the service provider will and won't do
- **Fee transparency:** How fees are calculated and who bears them (fund vs. management company)
- **Conflict management:** Disclosure of potential conflicts (especially for counsel)
- **LP expectations:** Institutional LPs expect to see professional service provider engagements during diligence
- **Liability allocation:** Limitation of liability, indemnification, and dispute resolution

### Who Pays?

| Service Provider | Typically Paid By | LPA Classification |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Formation counsel | Fund (up to org expense cap), then GP/management company | Organizational Expenses |
| Ongoing fund counsel | Fund | Fund Expenses |
| Fund administrator | Fund | Fund Expenses |
| Auditor | Fund | Fund Expenses |
| Placement agent | Fund or Management Company (varies) | Fund Expense or Management Company expense (check LPA) |
| Tax advisor | Fund | Fund Expenses |

## Outside Counsel Engagement {#outside-counsel}

### Template — Fund Formation Counsel

```
[LAW FIRM LETTERHEAD]

[Date]

[GP Entity Name], LLC
[Address]

Attention: [Managing Member Name]

Re: Engagement of [Law Firm Name] as Fund Formation Counsel

Dear [Name]:

Thank you for engaging [Law Firm Name] (the "Firm") to represent [GP Entity
Name], LLC (the "General Partner") and [Fund Name], L.P. (the "Fund") in
connection with the formation of the Fund and related matters. This letter
sets forth the terms of our engagement.

1. SCOPE OF ENGAGEMENT

The Firm will provide legal services in connection with the following
(collectively, the "Engagement"):

  (a) Formation of the Fund as a Delaware limited partnership, including
      preparation and filing of the Certificate of Limited Partnership;

  (b) Formation of the General Partner as a Delaware limited liability
      company, including preparation and filing of the Certificate of
      Formation;

  (c) Drafting and negotiation of the Fund's Agreement of Limited Partnership
      (the "LPA");

  (d) Drafting of the General Partner's Limited Liability Company Agreement;

  (e) Preparation of subscription documents, including the Subscription
      Agreement and Investor Questionnaire;

  (f) Preparation of template side letter provisions;

  (g) Preparation and filing of the Fund's Form D with the SEC;

  (h) [Drafting of the Management Company's operating agreement;]

  (i) [Preparation of placement agent agreement;]

  (j) General advice on fund formation matters, including regulatory
      compliance, ERISA considerations, and tax structuring, during the
      formation period.

The Engagement does not include: (i) ongoing fund operations legal support
beyond the formation period; (ii) portfolio company transaction work;
(iii) tax return preparation; (iv) regulatory filings beyond Form D;
(v) litigation or dispute resolution; or (vi) non-US legal advice.

2. CLIENT IDENTIFICATION

Our client in this Engagement is the General Partner. We do not represent
the Fund's Limited Partners, any individual principal of the General Partner
in their personal capacity, or any portfolio company.

We recognize that the General Partner and the Fund may have aligned but
potentially divergent interests. If a conflict arises between the General
Partner and the Fund, we will advise the General Partner and the Fund may
need to engage separate counsel.

3. FEES AND EXPENSES

Our fees for the Engagement will be billed on an [hourly basis at our
standard rates / flat fee basis as follows]:

[OPTION A — Hourly:]
  Partner:     $[●] per hour
  Associate:   $[●] per hour
  Paralegal:   $[●] per hour

We estimate total fees for the formation to be approximately $[●]–$[●],
depending on the complexity of negotiations and the number of side letters.
This estimate is not a cap.

[OPTION B — Flat Fee:]
  Formation package (LPA, GP Agreement, Sub Docs, Form D): $[●]
  Side letters: $[●] per side letter
  Additional services beyond scope: billed hourly at rates above

In addition to fees, the Fund will reimburse the Firm for reasonable
out-of-pocket disbursements, including filing fees, courier charges, and
travel expenses approved in advance.

4. BILLING AND PAYMENT

Invoices will be rendered [monthly / upon completion of milestones] and are
due within [30] days of receipt. Unpaid invoices will accrue interest at
[●]% per month.

5. DOCUMENT RETENTION

We will maintain files related to the Engagement in accordance with our
standard document retention policy. Upon termination of the Engagement, you
may request return of your documents. We may retain copies for our records.

6. CONFLICTS

We have conducted a conflicts check and are not aware of any conflict of
interest that would prevent us from undertaking this Engagement. We may
represent other investment funds, including funds with investment strategies
that overlap with the Fund's strategy. Our representation of the General
Partner and the Fund does not preclude us from representing other clients.

7. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

The Firm's aggregate liability to the General Partner and the Fund for any
claims arising out of the Engagement shall not exceed the total fees paid
to the Firm under this Engagement, except in cases of the Firm's willful
misconduct or gross negligence.

8. TERMINATION

Either Party may terminate this Engagement at any time upon written notice.
Upon termination, the Fund shall pay all fees and expenses incurred through
the date of termination.

9. GOVERNING LAW

This engagement letter shall be governed by the laws of the State of
[Delaware / New York].

Please confirm your agreement to these terms by signing and returning a copy
of this letter.

Very truly yours,

[LAW FIRM NAME]

By: ___________________________
Name: [Partner Name]

ACCEPTED AND AGREED:

[GP ENTITY NAME], LLC

By: ___________________________
Name: [●]
Title: [Managing Member]
Date: ___________________________
```

### Practitioner Notes — Outside Counsel

**Fee ranges (as of recent market):**
- Emerging manager fund formation (simple structure): $25,000–$75,000 flat
- Mid-market fund formation: $75,000–$200,000
- Large/complex fund formation (parallel funds, offshore feeders): $200,000–$500,000+
- Side letters: $2,000–$10,000 per letter depending on complexity

**Conflict disclosure:** Critical for large law firms that represent multiple funds. The engagement letter should explicitly note that the firm may represent other fund clients.

**Who is the client?** Always clarify. The client is the GP, not the LPs. LPs sometimes assume fund counsel represents their interests — the engagement letter should prevent this confusion.

## Fund Administrator / Auditor Engagement {#fund-admin}

### Template — Fund Administrator

```
FUND ADMINISTRATION SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Agreement is entered into as of [Date], by and between:

[Fund Administrator Name] (the "Administrator")
and
[GP Entity Name], LLC (the "General Partner"), on behalf of [Fund Name], L.P.
(the "Fund").

1. SERVICES

The Administrator shall provide the following services to the Fund:

  (a) Investor onboarding and AML/KYC processing
  (b) Capital call and distribution processing and notices
  (c) Maintenance of the Fund's books and records
  (d) Preparation of quarterly NAV calculations
  (e) Preparation of quarterly and annual financial statements (unaudited)
  (f) Investor reporting (capital account statements, portfolio summaries)
  (g) Coordination with the Fund's auditor for annual audit
  (h) K-1 preparation and distribution [or: coordination with tax preparer]
  (i) Regulatory filing support (Form PF, Form D updates)
  (j) Waterfall calculations for carried interest distributions
  (k) [Cash management / treasury services]
  (l) [ERISA monitoring and compliance testing]

2. FEES

[OPTION A — Fixed Annual Fee:]
  Annual administration fee: $[●] (payable [quarterly/monthly] in advance)
  Per-investor surcharge: $[●] per investor per year (for investors above [●])

[OPTION B — AUM-Based:]
  [●] basis points of Fund NAV per year, subject to a minimum fee of $[●]
  per year

Additional services outside the scope above will be billed at the
Administrator's standard hourly rates.

3. STANDARD OF CARE

The Administrator shall perform the Services with reasonable care and in
accordance with industry standards. The Administrator shall not be liable
for errors or omissions except to the extent caused by its negligence,
willful misconduct, or fraud.

4. INDEMNIFICATION

The Fund shall indemnify the Administrator against losses arising from the
Administrator's performance of the Services, except to the extent caused by
the Administrator's negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY

The Administrator shall maintain the confidentiality of all Fund and
investor information. The Administrator may disclose information as required
by law or regulation.

6. TERM AND TERMINATION

This Agreement shall continue until terminated by either Party upon [90]
days' prior written notice. Upon termination, the Administrator shall
cooperate in the orderly transition of services to a successor administrator.

7. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [State].

[Signature blocks]
```

### Practitioner Notes — Fund Admin

**Fee ranges:**
- Emerging manager funds (<$50M): $30,000–$75,000/year
- Mid-market funds ($50M–$500M): $75,000–$200,000/year
- Large funds: Negotiated, often AUM-based

**Common administrators for emerging managers:** Decile Partners, Standish Management, Juniper Square, Carta Fund Admin, Allvue, Apex Group.

**Why use one:** Institutional LPs expect third-party fund administration. Even for a $5M fund, the credibility benefit outweighs the cost. Fund admin also reduces GP operational burden and provides independent NAV verification.

### Auditor Engagement

Auditor engagement letters follow the AICPA standard format. Key points for fund audits:
- Auditor must be independent (no consulting services to the fund)
- Audit per GAAP (ASC 820 fair value for investments)
- Typical audit fee for emerging manager fund: $15,000–$40,000/year
- Big 4 auditors generally won't audit funds under $100M AUM; use reputable mid-tier firms (EisnerAmper, Citrin Cooperman, WithumSmith+Brown, KPMG Private Enterprise)

## Placement Agent Engagement {#placement-agent}

### Template — Placement Agent Agreement

```
PLACEMENT AGENT AGREEMENT

This Agreement is entered into as of [Date], by and between:

[Placement Agent Name], a [entity type] (the "Placement Agent")
and
[GP Entity Name], LLC (the "General Partner"), on behalf of [Fund Name], L.P.
(the "Fund").

1. APPOINTMENT

The General Partner hereby engages the Placement Agent on a [non-exclusive /
exclusive] basis to assist with the marketing and placement of limited
partnership interests in the Fund.

2. SERVICES

The Placement Agent shall:
  (a) Identify and introduce prospective Limited Partners
  (b) Assist in the preparation of marketing materials (subject to GP approval)
  (c) Coordinate investor meetings and presentations
  (d) Assist with investor due diligence processes
  (e) Provide market intelligence on investor appetite and competitive landscape

The Placement Agent shall not have authority to bind the General Partner or
the Fund, accept subscriptions, or make representations not contained in
Fund-approved materials.

3. COMPENSATION

(a) Placement Fee: [●]% of the capital commitments obtained from investors
    introduced by the Placement Agent (the "Placed Capital").

(b) Tail Period: The Placement Agent shall be entitled to the Placement Fee
    for commitments received from Placed Investors within [●] months following
    termination of this Agreement.

(c) Payment: Placement Fees shall be payable [upon closing of each
    commitment / in installments over [●] years as the capital is called].

(d) Fee Offset: Placement Fees shall be [100% / ●%] offset against the
    Management Fee payable by the Fund, in accordance with the LPA.

4. EXPENSES

[The General Partner / The Fund] shall reimburse the Placement Agent for
reasonable, pre-approved travel and marketing expenses, not to exceed $[●]
in aggregate.

5. REPRESENTATIONS AND COMPLIANCE

(a) The Placement Agent represents that it is [registered as a broker-dealer
    with the SEC and is a member of FINRA / exempt from broker-dealer
    registration pursuant to [applicable exemption]].

(b) The Placement Agent shall comply with all applicable laws, including the
    Securities Act of 1933, the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, and
    applicable state "blue sky" laws.

(c) The Placement Agent shall comply with the SEC's pay-to-play rules
    (Rule 206(4)-5 under the Investment Advisers Act) and shall not make
    political contributions that would restrict the Fund's ability to receive
    investments from government entities.

(d) The Placement Agent represents that it is not a "bad actor" as defined
    in Rule 506(d) of Regulation D.

6. DISCLOSURE

The General Partner shall disclose the Placement Agent's engagement and
compensation to all prospective investors and in the Fund's Form ADV and/or
offering documents, as required by applicable law.

7. TERM AND TERMINATION

This Agreement shall continue for a period of [●] months from the date
hereof, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon [30] days' written
notice. The Placement Fee and tail provisions shall survive termination.

8. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [State].

[Signature blocks]
```

### Practitioner Notes — Placement Agents

**Fee ranges:** 1–3% of placed capital is standard. 2% is most common.

**Regulatory requirements:** Placement agents must be registered broker-dealers (or fall within an exemption). Using an unregistered placement agent can disqualify the fund from Reg D safe harbors.

**Pay-to-play:** The SEC's pay-to-play rules (Rule 206(4)-5) restrict funds from receiving investments from government pension funds if the fund's placement agent made political contributions to officials with authority over the pension fund. This is a serious compliance risk.

**Disclosure to LPs:** SEC and FINRA require full disclosure of placement agent arrangements to investors. Failure to disclose is a common enforcement action.

**Fee offset:** LPA should specify whether placement fees offset management fees. 100% offset is market standard and LP-expected.

## GP Engaging Portfolio Companies {#portfolio-co}

### Template — Portfolio Company Services Agreement

```
SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Agreement is entered into as of [Date], by and between:

[Management Company Name], LLC (the "Service Provider")
and
[Portfolio Company Name], [Inc./LLC] (the "Company").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Service Provider is an affiliate of [GP Entity Name], LLC,
the general partner of [Fund Name], L.P. (the "Fund"), which holds an
investment in the Company;

WHEREAS, the Company desires to engage the Service Provider to provide
certain strategic, operational, and advisory services;

1. SERVICES

The Service Provider shall provide the following services to the Company:

  (a) Strategic advisory and board-level guidance
  (b) Assistance with fundraising strategy and investor introductions
  (c) Operational support including [financial planning / hiring /
      go-to-market strategy / technology architecture]
  (d) Introductions to potential customers, partners, and strategic contacts
  (e) [Interim executive services: [CFO / CTO / COO] support]
  (f) Such other services as mutually agreed

2. FEES

[OPTION A — No Separate Fee:]
The services are provided as part of the Fund's portfolio support and no
separate fee is charged. [Note: this is the cleanest structure for VC funds
and avoids conflict issues.]

[OPTION B — Fee-Based:]
The Company shall pay the Service Provider a [monthly / quarterly /
annual] fee of $[●] for the Services.

⚠️ If a fee is charged, (i) the fee must be disclosed to the Fund's LPs
and LPAC, (ii) the fee should be offset against the Fund's management fee
per the LPA, and (iii) the LPAC should consent to the arrangement.

3. TERM

This Agreement shall continue until [●] or until terminated by either
Party upon [30] days' written notice.

4. CONFLICT DISCLOSURE

The Company acknowledges that the Service Provider is affiliated with the
Fund, which holds an equity interest in the Company. The Service Provider's
advice may be influenced by the Fund's investment objectives. The Company
is encouraged to obtain independent advice on material decisions.

5. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

The Service Provider shall not be liable for any losses arising from the
Company's implementation of advice provided hereunder, except in cases of
the Service Provider's willful misconduct or gross negligence.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

Each Party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other's proprietary
information.

7. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [State].

[Signature blocks]
```

### Practitioner Notes — Portfolio Company Engagement

**Conflict management is key.** Any fees charged by the GP/management company to portfolio companies create a conflict of interest. Best practices:

- Disclose all portfolio company fees to LPs in the LPA and annual reporting
- Offset 100% of portfolio company fees against the management fee
- Obtain LPAC consent for material fee arrangements
- If no fee offset, expect LP pushback during diligence

**SEC scrutiny:** The SEC has brought enforcement actions against fund managers who charged undisclosed fees to portfolio companies. Full transparency is essential.

**No-fee model:** For emerging VC managers, the cleanest approach is to provide portfolio support at no additional charge, treating it as part of the value-add that justifies the management fee. This avoids conflict issues entirely.
